04 Terms used in this document

The following definitions explain every field and event recorded in this audit trail. This glossary is provided so the document can stand alone as a record of legal evidence in any subsequent proceeding.

01Audit trail
Also referred to as an attestation. A document that details specific information relating to each individual involved in the signing process, used as a record of legal evidence if required.
02Request
The process of preparing a document to be signed by one or more people.
03Proposer
The person — name and email address — who prepared the document and initiated the signature request.
04IP address
A unique address that identifies a device on the internet or a local network and can provide context as to the whereabouts of the signer.
05Request identifier
The unique reference number of the sealed document. With this ID, anyone can look up the document on sealed.app/verify, validate its authenticity, and obtain the audit trail and the original file.
06Signatory identifier
The unique identifier of the individual who signed the document. The UUID is linked only to the signature in the document referenced by the request ID on this audit trail.
07Digital signature
An additional layer of authenticity which adds a certificate to the signed document. A certificate indicates an embedded eIDAS qualified timestamp. Validity is revoked if the document is tampered with after signing.
08Verification check
Additional guarantees of identity the requester may enable for each signer. They include:
Email. The recipient's email is validated via a unique link. Access code. The signer is given a distinct code to open the document. SMS. A code is sent via text message prior to signing. ID verification. The signer presents a government-issued ID. Account. The signer is authenticated against a Sealed account.

05 Signature formats, events, and references
09Signature format
The visual style of the signature on the document. It can be typed (text), drawn by hand, or uploaded as an image.
10Events
Major actions taken by each participant.
Sent. The email request has been sent to the participant. Viewed. The participant has accepted the Terms and Privacy Policy and viewed the document. Signed. The signer has signed the document. Validated. The validator has validated the document.
11Participant
Anyone involved in the signing process. Roles:
Signatory. Required to sign the document. Validator. Given authority to read, validate, or reject before the signature process is finalized. Witness. Acknowledges the agreement took place. Does not interfere but can track and download.
12Qualified timestamp
A technological instrument that validates a document existed before a certain date and has not been modified since. Issued by an eIDAS trusted service provider.
13Hash (SHA-256)
A combination of letters and numbers generated by a cryptographic algorithm to produce a unique value that depends on the contents of the file. SHA-256 is the algorithm used here.
14Delivery mode
Parallel. All signers receive the request simultaneously. Sequential. Signers receive it in a defined order.
